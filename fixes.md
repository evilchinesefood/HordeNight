### 1. [HIGH] Grass + water stamp phantom geometry into the GTAO pre-pass
**Location:** Source/World/Vegetation.js (grass mesh), Source/World/Water.js (water mesh), Source/Engine/PostFx.js:36-43, Source/Main.js
**Problem:** GTAO's depth/normal pre-passes use override materials, dropping custom vertex shaders. The camera-wrap grass renders as an 11,000-instance static blob at the world origin (frustumCulled=false → submitted every frame); the transparent water plane renders as an opaque depth-writing 400m quad. Only clouds (layer 1) were excluded.
**Why it matters:** Wrong AO (dark smudges near the stream/origin, AO discontinuities along every shoreline, zero AO from real grass) plus ~132k wasted triangles/frame in the AO pass.
**Suggested fix:** Put grass and water on layer 2; enable layer 2 on the main camera; disable layers 1 AND 2 inside the patched gtao.render wrapper.
**Verification:** ?debug near the stream/origin — no dark AO smudge band; tris count in AO passes drops.

### 2. [HIGH] Impostor RNG draw-order desync (tint + height mismatch at LOD handoff)
**Location:** Source/World/Vegetation.js bucket loop (~:179-207)
**Problem:** Full tree consumes its Mulberry stream as ①rotation ②y-squash ③g ④leaf-red; impostor data re-creates the stream for ry (draw ① — correct by luck) but takes tint red from draw ⑤, and ignores the y-squash in its height. Verified numerically: red differs up to ~10%; silhouette height jumps ±8% at the 124m swap.
**Why it matters:** Visible hue/size pop at handoff; the "same stream, same draw order" invariant is undocumented and one inserted r() call from full desync.
**Suggested fix:** Draw once into locals (rot, ys, g, red) and build BOTH the full-tree matrix/color and the impostor record {ry: rot, h: impTop*sc*ys, tint} from them. Delete the second Mulberry construction.
**Verification:** Walk across the 124m handoff watching one tree — no hue or height jump.

### 4. [HIGH] heightTex Float32+LinearFilter needs a non-core WebGL2 extension
**Location:** Source/World/Terrain.js (DataTexture FloatType)
**Problem:** Linear filtering of 32-bit float textures requires OES_texture_float_linear (missing on a slice of GPUs, mostly mobile). Where absent, the texture is incomplete → grass collapses (density reads 0) and water depth reads 0 (all-foam).
**Why it matters:** Whole-feature failure invisible on dev machines.
**Suggested fix:** Convert to HalfFloatType (Uint16Array via THREE.DataUtils.toHalfFloat) — half-float linear filtering IS core WebGL2; precision (~±0.01) is ample. Also halves the texel bandwidth (perf L1).
**Verification:** npm test + visual: grass seats correctly, water depth gradient intact.

### 5. [MEDIUM] No stand-on-top collision → lateral pop when jumping onto low obstacles
**Location:** Source/Engine/Collision.js (resolvePlayer skip conditions), Source/Player/Player.js (grounding)
**Problem:** Colliders are skipped when feet clear their top; on descent inside the footprint, feet drop below the top and the lateral push-out pops the player sideways. Jump apex 1.31m vs ruin walls 0.7-2.7m, barrels/fences/rocks ~1m — reproducible everywhere.
**Why it matters:** Core movement feel (the spec's M1 bar) breaks on every jumpable prop.
**Suggested fix:** Step-band logic: lateral resolution skips colliders whose top is ≤ feet+0.35; grounding takes max(terrain height, highest overlapping collider top within the step band) → player can stand on walls/barrels/logs. Add unit tests.
**Verification:** New Tests/RunAll.js cases; in game, jump onto a ruin wall and stand stably.
